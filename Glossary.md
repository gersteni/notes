**[Causal Opacity](/causal-opacity.html)****:** It is impossible to know, for things above a very low bar of complexity, how they got that way or make accurate predictions about them. [^1]

**Defragilizing**: Improving something by removing an element that makes (or may make) it fragile.

**[Epiphenomenon](/epiphenomena-examples.html)**: A correlation mistaken for causation.

**[Fractal History](/fractal-history.html)**: The observation that historical causes are infinitely complex and interconnected, making simple causal explanations misleading.

**[Legibility](/legibility.html)**: The forced packaging of information so that an organization can understand it. [^2]

**[MECE](/mece.html)**: Mutually Exclusive, Collectively Exhaustive. When there’s a list of things that covers a topic, the list is [MECE](/mece.html) if it both covers everything in the topic, and none of the list items overlap.

**[Nonpredictive Planning](/nonpredictive-planning.html)**: Planning by setting goals and running experiments rather than making predictions about outcomes.

**[Opaque Heuristic](/opaque-heuristics.html)**: A practice that works but whose mechanism is not understood.

**[Translation Gap](/translation-gaps.html)**: The delay between when something useful is discovered and when it is actually put into practice.

**[Via Negativa](/via-negativa.html)****:** Taking things away. Latin for “negative way”.[^3]

**[Via Positiva](/via-positiva.html)****:** Adding things. Latin for “positive way”. [^4]
